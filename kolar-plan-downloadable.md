## PART A: MANDATORY BASELINE DIAGNOSTICS

**Without measured numbers, every dosage in this document is an educated guess. Execute before any intervention.**

### A1. Soil Analysis (0–30 cm within raised beds, 5 composites per acre)

| Parameter | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| pH (1:2 soil:water) | Determines nutrient availability thresholds |
| EC (saturated paste extract, dS/m) | Quantifies salt stress on roots |
| CEC (meq/100g) | Required for gypsum calculation |
| ESP (%) | The *only* accurate basis for gypsum dosing |
| Exchangeable Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Na⁺, K⁺ | Cation balance; Na displacement target |
| SAR | Long-term sodicity trajectory |
| Available P (Olsen method) | Olsen method is critical — Bray method underreads in alkaline soil |
| Organic Carbon % | Indicator of biological buffering capacity |
| Free CaCO₃ % | Determines lime-induced chlorosis risk |
| DTPA-extractable Fe, Zn, Mn, Cu | Plant-available micronutrient status |
| Bulk density (g/cm³) | Required for field-scale gypsum calculation |

### A2. Water Analysis (lake source — repeat quarterly)

| Parameter | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| pH, EC (dS/m), TDS | Baseline water quality |
| HCO₃⁻ and CO₃²⁻ (meq/L) | Determines acid injection rate |
| Cl⁻ (meq/L) | Avocado leaf Cl >0.25% causes chloroplast degradation and tip necrosis |
| Na⁺, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺ (meq/L) | For SAR calculation and Ca:Mg ratio |
| SO₄²⁻ | Determines whether sulfuric acid's S contribution is needed or redundant |
| SAR, RSC | Sodicity hazard and carbonate precipitation risk |

### A3. Leaf Tissue Analysis (biannual: March and September)

| Crop | Sampling Protocol | Optimal N Range |
|---|---|---|
| Avocado (Hass) | 5th–6th leaf from terminal on non-fruiting spring-flush shoots | 2.1–2.6% |
| Custard Apple (NMK Gold) | Fully expanded mature leaves from current growth | 1.8–2.2% |
| Guava (Japanese Red) | 3rd–4th leaf pair from shoot tip | 1.5–2.0% |

Analyze: N, P, K, Ca, Mg, S, Fe, Zn, Mn, Cu, B, **Na, Cl** (critical for salt monitoring)

**Hass optimal ranges (California Avocado Commission):** N: 2.1–2.6%, P: 0.12–0.15%, K: 0.9–1.2%, Zn: 50–150 ppm, Mn: 50–500 ppm, B: 50–100 ppm (CAUTION: >100 ppm causes necrosis), Fe: 60–200 ppm, Cl: <0.25%

Contact Indo Israel Avocado nursery to verify the **"Degania 169"** designation. Within the Degania line, different selections (62, 117, 169) exhibit vastly different Cl⁻ exclusion efficiencies. Obtain documented chloride exclusion data for the specific selection — this determines the orchard's survival ceiling under saline irrigation.

## PART C: WATER ACIDIFICATION

### C1. Acidification Strategy — Tiered Approach

The critical review recommends sulfuric acid for all acidification. This is scientifically correct for continuous irrigation but must be balanced against safety on a 5-acre farm with rural labor.

| Use Case | Recommended Acid | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Continuous irrigation acidification** (daily drip runs) | **Sulfuric acid 35%** via Dosatron injector | Citric acid biodegrades in soil within 12–24 hours — pH rebounds. H₂SO₄ is stable, provides sulfate-S, costs ~1/5th per meq neutralized |
| **Foliar spray tank** (same-day use) | **Citric acid** (dry granular) or **phosphoric acid 75%** | Acceptable for small volumes consumed within hours. Citric acid is safe to handle; phosphoric provides trace P |
| **Fertigation stock solution pH adjustment** | **Sulfuric acid 35%** or the acidifying fertilizer itself (Urea Phosphate has pH ~2.0 in solution) | Urea Phosphate self-acidifies; supplement with H₂SO₄ if additional neutralization needed |

### C2. Sulfuric Acid Injection Setup

**Hardware:**
- Dosatron proportional injector (acid-compatible model with EPDM or acid-resistant seals)
- HDPE drum (200L) for acid storage — locked, ventilated enclosure
- PPE: chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile or butyl), face shield, acid-resistant apron, emergency eyewash station

**SAFETY RULES (non-negotiable):**
1. Always add acid to water, NEVER water to acid
2. Only trained personnel handle acid — minimum 2 people present during refilling
3. Keep sodium bicarbonate (baking soda) nearby for emergency spill neutralization
4. Install Dosatron UPSTREAM of all fertigation injection points
5. Store acid away from fertilizers and organic materials
6. Post emergency procedures in Kannada at the injection point

**Calibration:**
1. Collect 20L lake water sample
2. Record baseline pH and alkalinity (HCO₃⁻ meq/L from lab)
3. Add 35% H₂SO₄ in 0.5 mL increments, stirring 60 seconds between
4. Target: pH 5.8–6.0 for irrigation, pH 5.5 for foliar spray tanks
5. Record mL acid per 20L → scale to daily irrigation volume → set Dosatron ratio
6. **Recalibrate quarterly** (lake alkalinity fluctuates with monsoon dilution)

**Alternative if sulfuric acid is not feasible:** If local safety infrastructure (emergency medical, trained handlers) is inadequate, use **phosphoric acid 75%** for continuous acidification as an intermediate step. Safer to handle. But monitor soil P carefully — excess P can precipitate iron, worsening Fe chlorosis.

### C3. Leaching Fraction

**LR = EC_w ÷ (5 × EC_e − EC_w)** where EC_e = 1.3 dS/m (avocado threshold)

Apply calculated LF once every 3–4 weeks (dry season) by extending irrigation run proportionally.

## PART D: GYPSUM REQUIREMENT — CALCULATED, NOT ESTIMATED

### D1. The Mathematics (ICAR-CSSRI Method)

**Step 1:** Na to replace (meq/100g) = CEC × [(Initial ESP − Target ESP) ÷ 100]

Target ESP = 5%

**Step 2:** Field-scale GR (tonnes/ha) = [Na to replace] × [soil depth cm] × [bulk density g/cm³] × 0.086 ÷ [gypsum purity fraction]

**Example:** CEC=25, ESP=18%, Target=5%, Depth=30cm, BD=1.5, Purity=70%
- Na to replace = 25 × 13/100 = 3.25 meq/100g
- GR = 3.25 × 30 × 1.5 × 0.086 ÷ 0.70 = **17.9 t/ha (7.25 t/acre)**

ICAR-CSSRI recommends: Apply 50% of GR in surface 15 cm, followed by leaching. Remainder over 2–3 seasons.

**Interim (until lab data):** Continue with 2 kg/tree estimate. Adjust immediately upon receiving ESP/CEC data.

### D2. Complementary Organic Amendment

Apply **20 t/ha (8 t/acre) well-decomposed FYM or vermicompost** alongside gypsum. ICAR-CSSRI demonstrates this enhances reclamation efficiency.

## PART E: FERTIGATION PROGRAM

### E1. Compatibility Rules (Non-Negotiable)

**NEVER co-inject or co-tank:**
- Calcium Nitrate + ANY phosphate → calcium phosphate precipitate
- Calcium Nitrate + ANY sulfate → calcium sulfate precipitate

**Protocol:** Sequential injection. Fertilizer A → flush 5 min → Fertilizer B. Or alternate days.

### E2. The Ratio Problem

The 8:4:2 LPH emitter system scales total *quantity* but delivers identical *ratios*. Species demands differ:

| Species | Critical NPK Ratio | Key Demand |
|---|---|---|
| Avocado (Year 2) | 1:1:1 or 2:1:2 | ~145g N/yr; balanced K for osmotic regulation |
| Custard Apple NMK Gold | 2:1:2 | Higher K for wood maturation |
| Guava Japanese Red | 3:1:1.5 | High N for post-deblossoming shoot elongation |

**Fix:** Drip solution handles avocado baseline. Every 3–4 weeks, deliver **supplemental hand-drenches**:
- Guava: 5g Urea Phosphate + 3g Urea per tree in 2L acidified water
- Custard Apple: 5g SOP per tree in 2L acidified water

### E5. AMF Management

Avocados lack root hairs — AMF dependency is extreme. Two practices suppress AMF:
1. High soil-available P (from Urea Phosphate) downregulates mycorrhizal signals
2. Mefenoxam drenches reduce AMF colonization

**Mitigation:** Time VAM inoculation 3–4 weeks AFTER fungicide treatments. If Olsen P >25 ppm, reduce P fertigation. Products: Tata Ralligold, Sun Bio VAM, Jaipur VAM (BigHaat).

## PART F: MICRONUTRIENT DELIVERY

### F1. The Avocado Foliar Feeding Problem (Independently Verified)

**Source:** Crowley et al. (1996), HortScience 31(2):224-229; UC ANR multiple publications.

SEM shows dense epicuticular wax on avocado leaves. Upper surface: zero stomates. ⁶⁵Zn studies: **<1% of foliar-applied zinc absorbed.** Over 96% of foliar ¹⁴C-urea recovered from leaf surface after 5 days.

**Critical finding the review document missed:** Crowley showed **Zn-EDTA via irrigation also FAILED** on pH 7.8 soil — EDTA dechelates above pH 7. What worked: **plain ZnSO₄ at 3.2 kg/tree via soil.**

### F2. Avocado Micronutrient Protocol — Soil-Applied

| Nutrient | Product | Rate/tree | Frequency | Method |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Iron** | Fe-EDDHA 6% (stable to pH 10) | 15g | 2×/year (Mar, Sep) | 5L acidified water, drip drench |
| **Zinc** | **ZnSO₄·7H₂O (NOT Zn-EDTA)** | 50g | 2×/year (Mar, Sep) | Dissolve in acidified water (pH 5.5), drip inject |
| **Manganese** | **MnSO₄ (NOT Mn-EDTA)** | 25g | 2×/year (Mar, Sep) | Dissolve in acidified water, drip inject |
| **Boron** | Solubor 20.5% — soil drench (vegetative) | 2g | 1×/year (Mar) | 5L water soil drench |
| **Boron (Year 3+)** | Solubor — foliar on inflorescences ONLY | 0.5 g/L | At "cauliflower" bloom stage | Tepals have functional stomates |

> **BORON CAUTION:** Avocado leaf B >100 ppm causes necrosis identical to salt burn — confounds diagnosis in saline environment.

### F3. Custard Apple + Guava — Foliar Spray (Effective)

These species have accessible stomates. Standard foliar works.

## PART G: FUNGICIDE PROGRAM

### G1. Phytophthora — Sink-Source Dynamics (Verified)

| Stage | Shoot Status | Phosphonate Goes To | Root Conc. |
|---|---|---|---|
| During spring flush | Strong SINK | Leaves (upward) | <9 mg/kg — INEFFECTIVE |
| 60–90 days post-flush | SOURCE | Roots (downward) | >28 mg/kg — EFFECTIVE |

**Apply Aliette/phosphonate ONLY after flushes mature (60–90 days post-bud break).**

### G2. Three-Way Chemical Rotation

| # | Timing | Product | FRAC | Rate | Method |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | May (60+ days post-spring flush) | **Aliette** (Fosetyl-Al 80% WP) | 33 | 3 g/L, 2–3 L/tree | Soil drench |

## PART M: EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### M1. Salt Burn (>30% avo canopy tip necrosis)
1. Leach at 150% volume immediately
2. SOP +50% for 2 weeks
3. Urgent leaf + soil EC analysis
4. EC_e >2.5: double gypsum, weekly leaching

### M2. Phytophthora (wilt without drought)
1. Check feeder roots: black, brittle = infection
2. Drench neighbors (10m radius) Aliette 3 g/L
3. No replant same hole 12+ months
4. Increase mulch to 6"
5. Audit irrigation

### M3. Iron Chlorosis
1. Emergency Fe-EDDHA 20 g/tree in 5L acidified water
2. Check soil pH; if >8.0, increase UP + elemental S
3. Verify acid injection functioning
