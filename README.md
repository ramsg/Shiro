## PART C: WATER ACIDIFICATION

### C1. Acidification Strategy — Tiered Approach

The critical review recommends sulfuric acid for all acidification. This is scientifically correct for continuous irrigation but must be balanced against safety on a 5-acre farm.

| Use Case | Recommended Acid | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Continuous irrigation acidification** (daily drip runs) | **Sulfuric acid 35%** via Dosatron injector | Citric acid biodegrades in soil within 12–24 hours — pH rebounds. H₂SO₄ is stable, provides sulfate-S, and costs ~1/5th as much per meq neutralized |
| **Foliar spray tank** (same-day use) | **Citric acid** (dry granular) or **phosphoric acid 75%** | Acceptable for small volumes consumed within hours. Citric acid is safe to handle; phosphoric provides trace P |
| **Fertigation stock solution pH adjustment** | **Sulfuric acid 35%** or the acidifying fertilizer itself (Urea Phosphate has pH ~2.0 in solution) | Urea Phosphate self-acidifies; supplement with H₂SO₄ if additional neutralization needed |

### C2. Sulfuric Acid Injection Setup

**Hardware:**
- Dosatron proportional injector (acid-compatible model with EPDM or acid-resistant seals)
- HDPE drum (200L) for acid storage — locked, ventilated enclosure
- PPE: chemical-resistant gloves (nitrile or butyl), face shield, acid-resistant apron, emergency eyewash station

**SAFETY RULES (non-negotiable):**
1. Always add acid to water, NEVER water to acid
2. Only trained personnel handle acid — minimum 2 people present during refilling
3. Keep sodium bicarbonate (baking soda) nearby for emergency neutralization of spills
4. Install Dosatron UPSTREAM of all fertigation injection points
5. Store acid away from fertilizers and organic materials
6. Post emergency procedures in Kannada at the injection point

**Calibration:**
1. Collect 20L lake water sample
2. Record baseline pH and alkalinity (HCO₃⁻ meq/L from lab)
3. Add 35% H₂SO₄ in 0.5 mL increments, stirring 60 seconds between
4. Target: pH 5.8–6.0 for irrigation, pH 5.5 for foliar spray tanks
5. Record mL acid per 20L → scale to daily irrigation volume → set Dosatron ratio
6. **Recalibrate quarterly** (lake alkalinity fluctuates with monsoon dilution)

**Alternative if sulfuric acid is not feasible:** If local safety infrastructure (emergency medical facilities, trained handlers) is inadequate, use **phosphoric acid 75%** for continuous acidification as an intermediate step. It's safer to handle and still provides stable pH suppression. However, monitor soil available P carefully — excess P from phosphoric acid can precipitate iron, worsening Fe chlorosis. Cap phosphoric acid use at the point where drip-delivered P exceeds crop demand.

### C3. Leaching Fraction

**LR = EC_w ÷ (5 × EC_e − EC_w)** where EC_e = 1.3 dS/m (avocado threshold)

Apply calculated LF once every 3–4 weeks (dry season) by extending irrigation run proportionally.

## PART D: GYPSUM REQUIREMENT — CALCULATED, NOT ESTIMATED

### D1. The Mathematics

**Step 1:** Na to replace (meq/100g) = CEC × [(Initial ESP − Target ESP) ÷ 100]
- Target ESP = 5%

**Step 2:** Field-scale GR (tonnes/ha) = [Na to replace] × [soil depth cm] × [bulk density g/cm³] × 0.086 ÷ [gypsum purity fraction]

**Example:** CEC=25, ESP=18%, Target=5%, Depth=30cm, BD=1.5, Purity=70%
- Na to replace = 25 × 13/100 = 3.25 meq/100g
- GR = 3.25 × 30 × 1.5 × 0.086 ÷ 0.70 = **17.9 t/ha (7.25 t/acre)**

ICAR-CSSRI recommends: Apply 50% of GR in surface 15 cm, followed by leaching. Remainder over 2–3 seasons.

**Interim (until lab data arrives):** Continue with 2 kg/tree estimate. Adjust immediately upon receiving ESP/CEC data.

### D2. Complementary Organic Amendment

Apply **20 t/ha (8 t/acre) well-decomposed FYM or vermicompost** alongside gypsum. ICAR-CSSRI has demonstrated this enhances reclamation efficiency by improving biological activity and physical structure.

## PART E: FERTIGATION PROGRAM

### E1. Compatibility Rules (Non-Negotiable)

**NEVER co-inject or co-tank:**
- Calcium Nitrate + ANY phosphate (UP, MAP, MKP, DAP) → calcium phosphate precipitate
- Calcium Nitrate + ANY sulfate (SOP, MgSO₄, AmSulf) → calcium sulfate precipitate

**Protocol:** Sequential injection. Fertilizer A → flush 5 min → Fertilizer B. Or alternate days.

### E2. The Ratio Problem — Why Unified Fertigation Alone Is Insufficient

The 8:4:2 LPH emitter system scales total nutrient *quantity* but delivers identical *ratios*. Species demands differ:

| Species | Critical NPK Ratio | Key Demand |
|---|---|---|
| Avocado (Year 2) | 1:1:1 or 2:1:2 | ~145g N/yr; balanced K for osmotic regulation |
| Custard Apple NMK Gold | 2:1:2 | Higher K for wood maturation and future fruit quality |
| Guava Japanese Red | 3:1:1.5 | High N for post-deblossoming shoot elongation |

**Fix:** Drip-delivered solution handles avocado baseline. Every 3–4 weeks, deliver **supplemental hand-drenches**:
- Guava: 5g Urea Phosphate + 3g Urea per tree in 2L acidified water (extra N+P)
- Custard Apple: 5g SOP per tree in 2L acidified water (extra K)

### E5. AMF (Mycorrhizal) Management

Avocados naturally lack root hairs — they depend on AMF for P, Zn, and Fe scavenging. Two management practices actively suppress AMF:

1. **High soil-available P** (from Urea Phosphate) downregulates plant signals attracting fungal partners
2. **Mefenoxam** (Ridomil Gold) soil drenches reduce AMF colonization

**Mitigation:** Time VAM inoculation 3–4 weeks AFTER fungicide treatments. Monitor soil available P — if Olsen P exceeds 25 ppm, reduce P fertigation intensity. Products: Tata Ralligold, Sun Bio VAM, Jaipur VAM (all BigHaat).

## PART F: MICRONUTRIENT DELIVERY

### F1. The Avocado Foliar Feeding Problem (Independently Verified)

**Source:** Crowley et al. (1996), HortScience 31(2):224-229; UC ANR multiple publications.

SEM studies show dense epicuticular wax on avocado leaves. Upper surface: zero stomates, continuous impermeable wax. Lower surface: low stomate density (350–510/mm²) shielded by wax guard cells.

Radiolabeled ⁶⁵Zn studies: **<1% of foliar-applied zinc is absorbed.** Over 96% of foliar-applied ¹⁴C-urea was recoverable from the leaf surface after 5 days. Virtually no translocation to other plant parts.

**Critical Crowley finding the review document missed:** Zn-EDTA applied via irrigation was also NOT effective on calcareous soil (pH 7.8). EDTA chelate is unstable above pH 7 — it dechelates and Zn precipitates. What DID work: **plain ZnSO₄ at 3.2 kg/tree via soil, applied quarterly or annually**, raising leaf Zn from <50 to 75–90 ppm.

**Implication:** For avocado on your pH >7.5 soil, the correct zinc source is **zinc sulfate via acidified drip water** — NOT Zn-EDTA (dechelates in your soil), and NOT foliar sprays (avocado leaves don't absorb them).

### F2. Avocado Micronutrient Protocol — Soil-Applied Via Drip

| Nutrient | Product | Rate/tree | Timing | Method |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Iron** | Fe-EDDHA 6% (EDDHA stable to pH 10) | 15g, 2×/year | March + September | Dissolve in 5L pH-adjusted water, drench via drip |
| **Zinc** | **Zinc Sulfate (ZnSO₄·7H₂O) — NOT Zn-EDTA** | 50g/tree, 2×/year | March + September | Dissolve in acidified water (pH 5.5), inject via drip during fertigation |
| **Manganese** | **Manganese Sulfate (MnSO₄) — NOT Mn-EDTA** | 25g/tree, 2×/year | March + September | Same — dissolve in acidified water, drip inject |
| **Boron** | Solubor 20.5% — **soil drench only** during vegetative phase | 2g/tree | March (once/year) | Dissolve in 5L water, soil drench |
| **Boron (flowering, Year 3+)** | Solubor — foliar on **inflorescences only** | 0.5 g/L at "cauliflower" stage | During bloom | Avocado inflorescence tepals have functional stomates and absorb B |

> **BORON CAUTION:** Avocado leaf B >100 ppm causes necrosis identical to salt burn — confounds diagnosis. Soil application at low rates provides controlled, steady uptake. Foliar B reserved exclusively for inflorescences during the brief flowering window.

## PART G: FUNGICIDE PROGRAM

### G1. Phytophthora cinnamomi — The Existential Avocado Threat

#### Phosphonate Sink-Source Dynamics (Verified: Whiley, Pegg, Queensland)

Phosphonate (Fosetyl-Al) movement within the avocado tree follows phloem mass flow — it goes wherever carbohydrates go:

| Stage | Shoot Status | Phosphonate Goes To | Root Concentration |
|---|---|---|---|
| During spring flush | Strong SINK | Leaves (upward) | **<9 mg/kg — INEFFECTIVE** |
| 60–90 days post-flush | SOURCE (mature leaves export carbs) | Roots (downward) | **>28 mg/kg — EFFECTIVE** |

**Critical rule:** Apply Aliette/phosphonate ONLY after vegetative flushes have matured (60–90 days post-bud break). Applying during active flush growth wastes the product.

#### Three-Way Chemical Rotation

| # | Timing | Product | FRAC Group | Rate | Method | Phenological Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | May (60+ days post-spring flush) | **Aliette** (Fosetyl-Al 80% WP) Bayer | 33 | 3 g/L, 2–3 L/tree | Soil drench | Flush matured → phosphonate reaches roots |
| 2 | Late June (early monsoon) | **Ridomil Gold** (Metalaxyl 4% + Mancozeb 64%) Syngenta | 4 + M03 | 2.5 g/L, 2–3 L/tree | Soil drench | Peak root infection risk |
| 3 | August (peak monsoon) | **Orondis Ultra** (Oxathiapiprolin 2.77% + Mandipropamid 23.08%) Syngenta | **49 + 40** | 0.7 mL/L | Soil drench or drip inject | New MOA — prevents resistance to Groups 4 and 33 |
| 4 | October | **Aliette** foliar spray | 33 | 3 g/L full canopy | Foliar | Post-monsoon flush matured |

> **ORONDIS ULTRA NOTE:** Registered in India (CIR-19493/2023) for grapes and selected vegetables. Use on avocado is technically off-label in India. Off-label use of registered pesticides is common practice in Indian agriculture but should be documented. The alternative Syngenta formulation **Sporium** (Oxathiapiprolin + Azoxystrobin, CIR-22936/2025) may have broader crop coverage — verify with Syngenta dealer.

> **Year 3+ consideration:** For trees >36 months, potassium phosphonate trunk injection (20% buffered solution) achieves far higher root concentrations than foliar spray. However, injection wounds on young avocado trees heal very slowly and can introduce Phytophthora — do NOT inject trees under 3 years old.

#### Aliette-Copper Incompatibility

**NEVER tank-mix Aliette with copper products. Minimum 14-day gap between applications.** The acidic Aliette solution reacts with ionic copper causing severe phytotoxicity.

### H1. Mealybug — Biocontrol First (Pollinator-Safe)

**The neonicotinoid conflict (verified):** Thiamethoxam translocates to pollen/nectar. Sublethal exposure causes 54% reduction in bee flight duration. This directly undermines the Hass/Ettinger cross-pollination design. EU banned all outdoor agricultural neonicotinoid use in 2018.

## PART I: PRUNING & CANOPY MANAGEMENT

### I1. Avocado (Year 2 — Structural Only)

- Remove water sprouts competing with central leader
- Remove narrow crotch angles (<30°)
- **Remove ALL premature flowers** immediately
- **DO NOT skirt** — lower branches protect bark from sunburn and shade shallow root zone
- Dip secateurs in 10% bleach between trees if any disease observed

### I2. Custard Apple NMK Gold (Solapur Method)

**Timing: February–March** (NOT May/June — pruning wounds need 8–10 weeks dry-season healing before monsoon fungal pressure)

- Head back central leader at ~90 cm
- Remove 30–40% previous terminal growth (fruiting wood develops on new growth from mature scaffolds)
- Thin interior: dead, diseased, crossing branches removed
- Goal: open-center vase for light and airflow

### I3. Guava Japanese Red (Post-Deblossoming)

**April (IMMEDIATE):**
- Chemical deblossoming: NAA (Planofix) at 50–100 ppm OR 10% Urea spray
- 6-month-old guava carrying fruit to maturity = permanent architectural stunting

**Training (Umbrella Method for Taiwan-type guavas):**
- Clean trunk below 60 cm
- When plant reaches 75–80 cm, cut back to 50–60 cm to force primary scaffolds
- Select 3–5 strong branches fanning around central stem
- Sep–Oct: Tip prune terminal 10 cm to stimulate secondary branching and winter fruiting wood

## PART M: EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### M1. Salt Burn (>30% avocado canopy tip necrosis)
1. Leach immediately at 150% volume
2. Increase SOP 50% for 2 weeks (K⁺ competes with Na⁺)
3. Urgent leaf tissue + soil EC analysis
4. If EC_e >2.5 dS/m: double gypsum, weekly leaching until <1.5

### M2. Phytophthora Detection (wilt without drought)
1. Check feeder roots: black, brittle, no white tips = infection
2. Drench ALL neighbors (10m radius) with Aliette 3 g/L immediately
3. Do NOT replant same hole for 12+ months
4. Increase mulch to 6"
5. Audit irrigation — over-watering is the #1 trigger

### M3. Iron Chlorosis (interveinal yellowing on young avocado leaves)
1. Emergency Fe-EDDHA drench: 20 g/tree in 5L acidified water
2. Review soil pH; if >8.0, increase UP and elemental sulfur
3. Verify acid injection system functioning

| Review Claim | Verification Outcome | Correction Applied |

| "Use soil-applied Zn-EDTA via drip" | ❌ **Crowley showed Zn-EDTA via irrigation FAILED on pH 7.8 soil** — EDTA dechelates above pH 7 | Corrected: Use ZnSO₄ (plain sulfate) in acidified water, not Zn-EDTA |
| Citric acid is "useless" | ⚠️ Overstated | Corrected: citric acid fails for continuous irrigation acidification (biodegrades in 12–24 hrs), but works fine for same-day spray tanks |
| Sulfuric acid for all acidification | ✅ Scientifically correct | Adopted with safety tier: H₂SO₄ for irrigation; citric/phosphoric for spray tanks. Safety protocol added for rural context. |

| Oxathiapiprolin (Orondis Ultra) in India | ✅ Registered (CIR-19493/2023) | Adopted with note: registered for grapes/veg, NOT specifically avocado. Off-label use documented. |
